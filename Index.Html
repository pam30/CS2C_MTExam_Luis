Hidden Garden of Vigan

The Hidden Garden of Vigan is a peaceful sanctuary tucked away from the busy streets of the city. It is known for its beautifully landscaped gardens filled with ornamental plants, bonsia, and native flowers. The place also features a cafe and restaurant that serves authentic Ilocano dishes such as bagnet, empanada, and longganisa. Visitors can also shop for souvenirs, plants, and local handicrafts while enjoying the relaxing atmosphere. The garden is designed to showcase both Ilocano culture and the natural beauty of the region. It has become a favorite stop for tourists looking for food, nature, and a calming escape in Vigan

Calle Crisologo

Calle Crisologo is the most famous street in Vigan, admired for its cobblestones road and centuries-old Spanish-era houses. The street becomes magical at the night when the antique lamps light up,creating a romantic and nostalgic atosphere. It is lined with souvenir shops, antique stores, and local eateries offering Ilocano delicacies. Visitors can take a leisurely stroll or ride a kalesa to enjoy the view of the historic mansions. Calle Crisologo reflects the gradeur of Vigan's history and is a favorite spots for photographers. It stands as a living museum that keeps the colonial past alive.

Bantay Bell Tower

The Bantay Bell Tower is a historic watchtower and belfry located on hill near the Bantay Church. It was built in 1591 and served as a lookout point during wars and invasions in Ilocos Sur. The tower is made of red bricks and offers a commanding view of Vigan and its surrounding landscapes. Visitors can climb up the tower to enjoy a panoramic view of the town, mountains, and coastline. It is also associated with folklore and legends, making it both a cultural and historical landmark. Today,Bantay Bell Tower is one of the most photographed icons of ilocos sur.

Ilocos Vinegar

₱120

Ilocos Vinegar, or sukang Iloko, is a traditional vinegar made from naturally fermented sugarcane juice. It carries a bold,tangy flavor with a subtle sweetness thta enhances any Filipino dish. Generations of Ilocanos have used it as a staple condiment, especially for empanada, bagnet, and grilled specialties. Its slow fermentation process gives it a unique aroma and authentic taste that makes it stand out from ordinary vinegar. Truly, Ilocos Vinegar is a taste of heritage in every drop.

Salted Fish(Bagoong)

₱100

Bagoong, or Salted fish, is a beloved Ilocano delicacy ade from fermented fish or shrimp. It has a strong, savory flavor that perfectly complements many traditional dishes, such as pinakbet, and green mangoes. known for its rich umami taste, bagoong is a staple in Ilocano households and kitchens across the Philippines. The careful fermentation process gives it a distinct aroma and authenticity passed down through generations. More than just a condiment, bagoong represents the Ilocano love for Bold and flavorful cuisine.
